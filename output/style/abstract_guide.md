# Abstract Writing Guide for Nature Communications

## Tense Usage
- Background: Present tense (约80%)
- Methods: Past tense (约20%)
- Results: Present tense
- Conclusions: Present tense

## Structure
1. Background/Purpose (1-2 sentences)
2. Methods (1 sentence)
3. Results (2-3 sentences, include key findings and statistics)
4. Conclusions (1-2 sentences, clinical/research implications)

## Key Vocabulary (Top 40)
### Nouns: soil, warming, figure, temperature, soils, emissions, effect, fluxes, mineralization, nitrification, denitrification, cycling, effects, availability, plants, root, ratio, abundance, genes, rates
### Verbs: increase, warm, wet, dry, download, see, govern, induce, affect, show
### Adjectives: microbial, elevated, higher, organic, high, applicable, global, significant, whole, low

## Sentence Structure
- Average length: 21.46 words
- Sentence types: Simple 40%, Compound 30%, Complex 30%
- Length distribution: Short 30%, Medium 50%, Long 20%

## Style Notes
- Length: 150-250 words (check journal requirements)
- No citations or abbreviations (unless defined)
- Write in complete sentences, avoid bullet points
- Passive voice ratio: 0%
- Focus on most important findings first
- Use coordinating conjunctions sparingly, prefer complex sentences
